## Candidates

### 1. World Mouse: Exploring Interactions with a Cross-Reality Cursor

- **Authors:** Esen K. Tütüncü, Mar Gonzalez-Franco, Khushman Patel, Eric J. Gonzalez (Google)
- **Venue / Date:** arXiv 2603.10984 (2026-03-11); CHI '26 companion work
- **URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.10984
- **DOI:** 10.48550/arXiv.2603.10984
- **Hook:** Reinterprets the 2D desktop mouse as a cross-reality cursor — uses surface normals for within-object precision, interpolation for between-object navigation, and semantic-segmentation + mesh reconstruction to treat physical objects as interactive surfaces.
- **Why-matched:** Rethinks fundamental XR input — direct counter-argument to the "natural inputs only" orthodoxy (touch fatigues, gaze is imprecise). Google team with consistent XR output.
- **Status:** new

### 2. SwEYEpinch: Intuitive, Efficient Text Entry for XR via Eye and Hand Tracking

- **Authors:** Ziheng (Leo) Li, Paul Sajda (Columbia) et al.
- **Venue / Date:** CHI '26, Barcelona — arXiv 2604.03520 (2026-04-03)
- **URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.03520
- **DOI:** 10.1145/3772318.3791820
- **Hook:** Gaze swipes across a virtual keyboard (the *where*) + manual pinch held throughout the swipe (the *when*); spatiotemporal DTW decoder with mid-swipe prediction and in-gesture cancellation. 30-session longitudinal study peaks at **64.7 WPM**.
- **Why-matched:** XR text entry is the perennial unsolved bottleneck; this is a numerically strong result (>60 WPM is ≈ keyboard-range) with a clean decoupling principle.
- **Status:** new

### 3. How Do We Evaluate Experiences in Immersive Environments?

- **Authors:** Xiang Li, Wei He, Per Ola Kristensson (Cambridge)
- **Venue / Date:** CHI '26, Barcelona — arXiv 2601.17811 v2 (2026-02-04)
- **URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.17811
- **DOI:** 10.1145/3772318.3790724
- **Hook:** Bottom-up scoping review of **375 XR papers** from CHI, UIST, VRST, SUI, IEEE VR, ISMAR, TVCG — maps what gets measured, how, and where the evaluation practice is breaking down. Forward agenda for XR UX evaluation.
- **Why-matched:** Orienting read for anyone running or reading XR user studies; calibrates what's standard vs novel. High-signal venue (CHI '26 main track).
- **Status:** new

### 4. Vibe Coding XR: Accelerating AI + XR Prototyping with XR Blocks and Gemini

- **Authors:** Ruofei Du, Benjamin Hersh, David Li, Nels Numan, Xun Qian, Yanhe Chen, Zhongyi Zhou et al. (Google)
- **Venue / Date:** arXiv 2603.24591 v2 (2026)
- **URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.24591
- **DOI:** 10.48550/arXiv.2603.24591
- **Hook:** End-to-end LLM-driven prototyping loop on top of XR Blocks — natural-language intent → WebXR headset deployment. Introduces VCXR60, a 60-prompt XR benchmark.
- **Why-matched:** Direct successor to the **XR Blocks** white paper already in the KB (`[[XR - XR Blocks]]`) — operationalizes the "Reality Model + vibe coding" thesis into a concrete workflow and benchmark. Closes the loop started by the prior ingest.
- **Status:** new — close companion to `[[XR - XR Blocks]]`

### 5. PeriphAR: Fast and Accurate Real-World Object Selection with Peripheral AR Displays

- **Authors:** Yutong Ren, Arnav Reddy, Michael Nebeling (U. Michigan)
- **Venue / Date:** CHI '26, Barcelona — arXiv 2603.18350 (2026-03-18)
- **URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.18350
- **DOI:** 10.1145/3772318.3791637
- **Hook:** Visualization technique that leverages peripheral vision for gaze-driven object selection on monocular AR glasses; emulated on Quest Pro with degraded FOV/rendering to simulate the target hardware class.
- **Why-matched:** One of the few CHI '26 papers targeting the *monocular* smart-glasses form factor rather than full HMDs — where the market (Meta Orion, Ray-Ban) is actually heading.
- **Status:** new

### 6. StylusPort: Investigating Teleportation using Stylus in VR

- **Authors:** Yang Liu, Qiushi Zhou, Mathias N. Lystbæk, Aidan Kehoe, Mario Gutierrez, Hans Gellersen, Ken Pfeuffer
- **Venue / Date:** CHI '26, Barcelona — arXiv 2602.21799 (2026-02-25)
- **URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.21799
- **DOI:** 10.1145/3772318.3793420
- **Hook:** Teleportation techniques driven by a 6-DoF stylus (Meta MX Ink) + gaze, with two mode-switching methods (Button, Tilt). Addresses the awkward stylus-drops-when-you-teleport workflow in VR design/sketch tools.
- **Why-matched:** Narrow but sharp — targets the growing "stylus-in-VR for 3D design" niche that the Meta MX Ink launched in 2025.
- **Status:** new

### 7. Geometry Aware Passthrough Mitigates Cybersickness

- **Authors:** Trishia El Chemaly (Stanford), Mohit Goyal, Tinglin Duan, Vrushank Phadnis, Sakar Khattar, Bjorn Vlaskamp, Achin Kulshrestha, Eric Lee Turner, Aveek Purohit, Gregory Neiswander, Konstantine Tsotsos (Google)
- **Venue / Date:** arXiv 2502.11497 v2 (2025-03-25); CHI-track preprint
- **URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.11497
- **DOI:** 10.48550/arXiv.2502.11497
- **Hook:** Introduces **Geometry Aware Passthrough (GAP)** — depth-based reprojection algorithms for video see-through HMDs — and shows through a user study that GAP significantly reduces nausea, disorientation, and total cybersickness scores vs. direct passthrough.
- **Why-matched:** Passthrough correctness is the unresolved comfort problem for every MR headset shipping today (Quest 3, Vision Pro). Connects directly to `[[XR - Depth Reconstruction for Space-Warp]]` already in the KB.
- **Status:** new

### 8. Searching Through Complex Worlds: Visual Search and Spatial Regularity Memory in Mixed Reality

- **Authors:** Lefan Lai, Tinghui Li, Zhanna Sarsenbayeva, Brandon Victor Syiem (U. Melbourne)
- **Venue / Date:** CHI '26, Barcelona — arXiv 2602.18669 (2026-02-21)
- **URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.18669
- **DOI:** 10.1145/3772318.3790976
- **Hook:** Empirical MR study on how environment complexity, spatial regularity, and visual depth affect users' visual-search performance and workload in dual-task settings.
- **Why-matched:** Perceptual/cognitive baseline paper — the kind of result downstream MR UI designers cite when choosing where to place notifications/menus.
- **Status:** new

### 9. A Multi-View 3D Telepresence System for XR Robot Teleoperation

- **Authors:** Enes Ulas Dincer, Manuel Zaremski, Alexandra Nick, Elias Wucher, Barbara Deml, Gerhard Neumann (KIT)
- **Venue / Date:** arXiv 2604.03730 (2026-04-04)
- **URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.03730
- **DOI:** 10.48550/arXiv.2604.03730
- **Hook:** GPU-accelerated point-cloud rendering on standalone Quest hardware (up to 75k points real-time), fused from three cameras + a wrist-mounted RGB stream for high-res local detail. YOLOv11 semantic filtering. Runs on Meta Quest class silicon.
- **Why-matched:** Canonical XR-for-robotics piece — the intersection (teleop + spatial video) is heating up (OPEN TEACH, IRIS, LeVR all within past year). Shows what actually fits in a 90-Hz standalone budget.
- **Status:** new

### 10. On the Promises and Challenges of AI-Powered XR Glasses as Embodied Software

- **Authors:** Ruizhen Gu, Jingqiong Zhang, Jose Miguel Rojas, Donghwan Shin (U. Sheffield)
- **Venue / Date:** AIware '25 — 2025-10-21
- **URL:** https://ruizhengu.github.io/assets/files/AIware-main-12.pdf
- **DOI:** 10.1145/3744916.3764582 *(best-guess AIware '25 DOI stem — not yet indexed; record as `forthcoming` and confirm at ingest-time)* · arXiv not located.
- **Hook:** Positions the emerging **AI-powered XR glasses** category (Meta smart glasses + spatial-computing HMDs) as a new software-engineering target: three-layer distributed architecture, OS-level vs app-level FM agents, security/privacy/validation research problems.
- **Why-matched:** Software-engineering lens on XR — complements the HCI-heavy stack above. Flags the validation + privacy gap that `[[XR - XR Blocks]]` and Vibe Coding XR both assume away.
- **Status:** new

### 11. Towards spatial computing: recent advances in multimodal natural interaction for XR headsets

- **Authors:** Zhimin Wang, Maohang Rao, Shanghua Ye, Weitao Song, Feng Lu (Beihang)
- **Venue / Date:** Frontiers of Computer Science 2025, Vol 19(12); arXiv 2502.07598 v2 (2025-06-13)
- **URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.07598 · https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11704-025-41123-8
- **DOI:** 10.1007/s11704-025-41123-8
- **Hook:** Systematic review of multimodal natural interaction for wearable XR (2022–2024) drawn from CHI, UIST, IMWUT, IEEE VR, ISMAR, TVCG. Categorizes by application scenario, operation type, and — critically — how AI/LLMs are entering the interaction loop.
- **Why-matched:** Map before the expedition — indexes the papers behind most of the ones above. Cheap accelerator for any subsequent distil in this area.
- **Status:** new
