## Candidates (10)

### 1. Design Principles & Issues for Gaze and Pinch Interaction — Pfeuffer 2024  ⭐ top pick
- **Authors:** Ken Pfeuffer (Aarhus University)
- **Venue / Date:** arXiv preprint of an IEEE Computer Graphics and Applications article, January 2024 (also Lancaster ePrint copy, July 2024 final version)
- **URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.10948 · also https://eprints.lancs.ac.uk/id/eprint/223408/1/CGA_24_Design_Principles_2_.pdf
- **DOI:** 10.48550/arXiv.2401.10948 (preprint); CG&A version DOI not yet confirmed
- **Hook:** Directly addresses gaze + pinch as deployed in Apple Vision Pro — five design principles ("eyes select, hands manipulate"; minimalistic multimodal timing; flexible gesture support; infallible eyes; compatibility with hand-based interfaces) and the open issues including **control-display ratio for distant dragging** and drag-and-drop sequence problems when the user has to re-engage gaze to relocate a dropped object.
- **Why matched:** Singularly on-target — explicitly discusses how 1:1 mapping "feels very slow" for distant objects and proposes amplifying transfer-function gain with object distance (or using visual angle as a distance-independent metric). This is the design rationale behind visionOS's z-axis telescoping.
- **Status:** new

### 2. Gaze + Pinch Interaction in Virtual Reality — Pfeuffer et al. 2017  ⭐ top pick
- **Authors:** Ken Pfeuffer, Benedikt Mayer, Diako Mardanbegi, Hans Gellersen
- **Venue / Date:** ACM Symposium on Spatial User Interaction (SUI '17), Brighton, UK, October 2017
- **URL:** https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3131277.3132180 · open PDF: https://3dvar.com/Pfeuffer2017Gaze.pdf
- **DOI:** 10.1145/3131277.3132180
- **Hook:** Foundational paper for the "gaze targets, pinch acts" technique that Apple Vision Pro adopted seven years later. Establishes the gaze-as-cursor + indirect freehand-gesture-as-actuation pattern, including translate, scale, and rotate on objects at any distance.
- **Why matched:** This is the canonical reference for the AVP interaction model. To understand why drag in visionOS works the way it does, you need this paper.
- **Status:** new

### 3. At a Glance to Your Fingertips: SightWarp — Liu, Mikkelsen, Liu, Tian, Mardanbegi, Zhou, Gellersen, Pfeuffer 2025
- **Authors:** Yang Liu, Thorbjørn Mikkelsen, Zehai Liu, Gengchen Tian, Diako Mardanbegi, Qiushi Zhou, Hans Gellersen, Ken Pfeuffer
- **Venue / Date:** ACM UIST '25 (38th Symposium on User Interface Software and Technology), Sept–Oct 2025
- **URL:** https://arxiv.org/html/2508.04821 · arxiv 2508.04821
- **DOI:** 10.48550/arXiv.2508.04821 (preprint); UIST DOI pending
- **Hook:** Critique of gaze-and-pinch as *indirect* and lacking proprioceptive feedback at distance; SightWarp summons a scaled object proxy to the user's fingertips when their gaze shifts from the distant target to their hand, then enables direct manipulation.
- **Why matched:** A direct response to the limits of AVP-style indirect dragging at distance. Same Pfeuffer/Gellersen lineage. Demonstrates a successor pattern relevant to "how do I drag a window that's 5 metres away."
- **Status:** new

### 4. The Benefits of Near-field Manipulation and Viewing to Distant Object Manipulation in VR — Babu, Hsieh, Chuang 2024  ⭐ top pick
- **Authors:** Sabarish V. Babu (Clemson), Wei-An Hsieh, Jung-Hong Chuang (NYCU)
- **Venue / Date:** IEEE VR 2024, Orlando, March 2024 (pp. 408–417)
- **URL:** https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/10494146/ · open PDF: https://cggm.cs.nycu.edu.tw/images/Researches/2024_The%20Benefits%20of%20Near-field%20Manipulation%20and%20Viewing%20to%20Distant%20Object%20Manipulation%20in%20VR/IEEE_VR_2024_1663_Near_field_Viewing_and_Manipulation_Final_Version-PassedValidation%201.pdf
- **DOI:** 10.1109/VR58804.2024.00062
- **Hook:** Enhances two distant-object techniques — Bimanual Near-Field Metaphor with Scaled Replica (BMSR) and Scaled HOMER — by giving users a reach-space replica of the target *and its context* to manipulate directly, instead of dragging the distant object via raycast. Direct BMSR and Scaled HOMER+NFSRV both significantly outperform their baselines on accuracy.
- **Why matched:** Explicitly tackles the "drag a far-away thing" problem with a scale-and-bring-close pattern that mirrors what AVP does informally with z-axis telescoping. State-of-the-art for evaluating these designs.
- **Status:** new

### 5. PRISM Interaction for Enhancing Control in Immersive Virtual Environments — Frees, Kessler, Kay 2007  ⭐ top pick
- **Authors:** Scott Frees, G. Drew Kessler, Edwin Kay
- **Venue / Date:** ACM Transactions on Computer-Human Interaction (TOCHI), Vol. 14 No. 1, May 2007 (extending IEEE VR 2005 conference paper, DOI 10.1109/VR.2005.46)
- **URL:** https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/1229855.1229857
- **DOI:** 10.1145/1229855.1229857
- **Hook:** The canonical *speed-adaptive control-display ratio* technique. Slow hand → CD ratio raised → fine-grained, instability-filtered manipulation. Fast hand → CD ratio falls back to 1:1 → direct, unconstrained motion. This is the algorithmic ancestor of every "feels precise when you're being precise" XR drag.
- **Why matched:** The user wants to understand *how* dragging works algorithmically. PRISM is the textbook answer for variable CD-gain, on which AVP's drag and Quest's pinch-drag both implicitly draw.
- **Status:** new

### 6. Distant Object Manipulation with Adaptive Gains in Virtual Reality — 2022
- **Authors:** Liu et al. (X. Liu et al.)
- **Venue / Date:** IEEE ISMAR 2022, Singapore, October 2022 (venue inferred from search; verify on IEEE Xplore)
- **URL:** https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/9994878/
- **DOI:** likely 10.1109/ISMAR55827.2022.xxxxx — verify
- **Hook:** Adaptive-gain extension of distant-object manipulation: the gain on the user's hand-to-object mapping varies with task context (distance, speed) to balance reach-extension and precision.
- **Why matched:** Companion to PRISM — same idea (gain adaptation) but applied directly to the *distant* manipulation case, which is the case AVP/Quest have to solve for windows away from arm's reach.
- **Status:** new

### 7. Advantages of Velocity-Based Scaling for Distant 3D Manipulation — Frees, Kessler 2005
- **Authors:** Scott Frees, G. Drew Kessler
- **Venue / Date:** Eurographics / IEEE VR 2005-era; ResearchGate copy widely available
- **URL:** https://www.researchgate.net/publication/221314471_Advantages_of_velocity-based_scaling_for_distant_3D_manipulation
- **DOI:** 10.1145/1101616.1101633 (probable — verify; ACM VRST 2005 paper)
- **Hook:** Earlier velocity-based scaling proposal that informed PRISM. Demonstrates that scaling input velocity into output displacement specifically helps when the manipulated object is *far*, because hand jitter dominates angular accuracy.
- **Why matched:** Historical companion to PRISM, focused on the distant case the user cares about.
- **Status:** new

### 8. How to Evaluate Object Selection and Manipulation in VR? Guidelines from 20 Years of Studies — Bergström, Dalsgaard, Alexander, Hornbæk 2021
- **Authors:** Joanna Bergström, Tor-Salve Dalsgaard, Jason Alexander, Kasper Hornbæk
- **Venue / Date:** ACM CHI '21, Yokohama, May 2021
- **URL:** https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3411764.3445193 · open PDF: https://3dvar.com/Bergstr%C3%B6m2021How.pdf
- **DOI:** 10.1145/3411764.3445193
- **Hook:** Methodology survey of 20 years of VR selection-and-manipulation studies, synthesised into a checklist for designing and reporting comparable evaluations of techniques like pinch-drag.
- **Why matched:** Background reading for *how to think about* whether a drag technique is good — useful when comparing AVP, Quest, and any custom design.
- **Status:** new

### 9. Everything to Gain: Combining Area Cursors with Increased Control-Display Gain for Fast and Accurate Touchless Input — 2025
- **Authors:** (not extracted from search; CHI '25 paper)

- **URL:** https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3706598.3714021

- **Why matched:** Most recent CD-gain study in a touchless context — directly applicable to designing the "feel" of pinch-drag at distance.

### 10. Interface Design and Interaction Optimisation for Spatial Computing on Apple Vision Pro — 2025
- **Authors:** (Frontiers in Computer Science 2025; authors not extracted)
- **Venue / Date:** Frontiers in Computer Science, 2025

## Top Picks

1. **[#1] Pfeuffer 2024 — Design Principles & Issues for Gaze and Pinch.** Reads like a designer's manual for the AVP interaction model, including the dragging/CD-ratio issue the user is asking about. This is the single most direct answer in the feed.
2. **[#4] Babu, Hsieh, Chuang 2024 — Near-field Manipulation for Distant Objects (IEEE VR).** Recent, rigorous, and explicitly about the "drag at variable distance" problem. Pairs the scaled-replica idea with the bring-close gesture that AVP also uses.
3. **[#5] Frees, Kessler, Kay 2007 — PRISM (TOCHI).** The algorithmic foundation for variable CD-gain dragging. Without this, you can't reason properly about why AVP drags feel different up close vs. far away.

Honourable mention: **#2 SUI '17 Gaze + Pinch** as the historical predecessor everyone cites, and **#3 SightWarp UIST '25** as the most interesting recent twist on the same problem space.
