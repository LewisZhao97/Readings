## Candidates (ranked)

### 1. ViPS: Video-informed Pose Spaces for Auto-Rigged Meshes
- **Authors:** Honglin Chen, Karran Pandey, Rundi Wu, Matheus Gadelha, Yannick Hold-Geoffroy, Ayush Tewari, Niloy J. Mitra, Changxi Zheng, Paul Guerrero (Adobe / UCL / Columbia)
- **arXiv:** 2604.17623 — https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.17623
- **DOI:** 10.48550/arXiv.2604.17623
- **Hook:** Feed-forward framework that distils motion priors from a *pretrained video diffusion model* into a universal pose-space distribution for any auto-rigged mesh, eliminating dependence on scarce artist-authored 4D data. Zero-shot generalization to unseen species and skeletal topologies; differentiable validators enforce no self-intersection without manual regularizers.
- **Why matched:** Generative 3D rigging / pose manifolds — adjacent to the neural-rendering topic and a new flavor of "distil video priors into 3D structure." Strong team.
- **Status:** new

### 2. Instant Colorization of Gaussian Splats
- **Authors:** Daniel Lieber, Alexander Mock, Nils Wandel
- **arXiv:** 2604.17155 — https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.17155
- **DOI:** 10.48550/arXiv.2604.17155
- **Hook:** Solves the *2D → 3D* mapping for Gaussian Splatting (feeding color/features/segmentation back onto an existing splat scene) via a visibility-weighted least-squares normal equation per Gaussian. Up to an order of magnitude faster than gradient-descent baselines; enables relighting, stylization, and 3D semantic segmentation.
- **Why matched:** Sits cleanly on the `[[Graphics - Neural Rendering]]` axis; a fast, practical tool rather than yet-another-splat-training paper.
- **Status:** new

### 3. Asset Harvester: Extracting 3D Assets from Autonomous Driving Logs for Simulation
- **Authors:** Tianshi Cao, Jiawei Ren, Yuxuan Zhang, Jaewoo Seo, Jiahui Huang, Shikhar Solanki, Haotian Zhang, Mingfei Guo, Haithem Turki, Muxingzi Li, Yue Zhu, Sipeng Zhang, Zan Gojcic, Sanja Fidler, Kangxue Yin (NVIDIA, Toronto)
- **arXiv:** 2604.18468 — https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18468
- **DOI:** 10.48550/arXiv.2604.18468
- **Hook:** Image-to-3D pipeline that converts sparse in-the-wild object observations from real AV logs into complete simulation-ready assets. Couples sparse-view-conditioned multiview generation with 3D Gaussian lifting; system-level paper, not a single-model paper.
- **Why matched:** NVIDIA/Fidler team; shipping-oriented system design (compare with Mob-FGSR's academic-to-production story). Useful as an exemplar of "neural reconstruction that survives real-world heterogeneous sensor data."
- **Status:** new

### 4. PCM-NeRF: Probabilistic Camera Modeling for Neural Radiance Fields under Pose Uncertainty
- **Authors:** Shravan Venkatraman, Rakesh Raj Madavan, Pavan Kumar Sathya Venkatesh
- **arXiv:** 2604.17831 — https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.17831
- **DOI:** 10.48550/arXiv.2604.17831
- **Hook:** Per-camera learnable mean + variance over SfM poses, initialized from correspondence quality; uncertainty modulates the effective pose learning rate so poorly initialized cameras can't corrupt the reconstruction. Drop-in on SG-NeRF.
- **Why matched:** Small, elegant trick with a clear theme ("use uncertainty as an optimization regularizer") that generalizes beyond NeRF. Touches the neural-rendering topic.
- **Status:** new

### 5. Amortized Inverse Kinematics via Graph Attention for Real-Time Human Avatar Animation (IK-GAT)
- **Authors:** Muhammad Saif Ullah Khan, Chen-Yu Wang, Tim Prokosch, Michael Lorenz, Bertram Taetz, Didier Stricker
- **arXiv:** 2604.16629 — https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.16629
- **DOI:** 10.48550/arXiv.2604.16629
- **Hook:** Graph-attention network over the skeletal parent-child graph reconstructs full joint orientations from sparse 3D positions in one forward pass. 374 K params, **650+ FPS on CPU**, geodesic-on-SO(3) loss, 6D rotation parameterization. Directly drives SMPL-compatible rigs.
- **Why matched:** Real-time XR / avatar animation; mobile-CPU-friendly (fits the mobile-real-time thread from Mob-FGSR and the latency-sensitive Space-Warp thread).
- **Status:** new

### 6. Training and Agentic Inference Strategies for LLM-based Manim Animation Generation
- **Authors:** Ravidu Suien Rammuni Silva, Ahmad Lotfi, Isibor Kennedy Ihianle, Golnaz Shahtahmassebi, Jordan J. Bird
- **arXiv:** 2604.18364 — https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18364
- **DOI:** 10.48550/arXiv.2604.18364
- **Hook:** Unified study of SFT + GRPO training with Renderer-in-the-Loop (RITL) + API-doc-augmented RITL inference for text→code→Manim-video. Qwen 3 Coder 30B reaches 94% Render Success / 85.7% Visual Similarity, beating GPT-4.1 baseline. Evaluates 17 sub-30B open models on ManimBench.
- **Why matched:** Sits at the intersection of `[[LLM - Harness Engineering]]` and `[[LLM - Code Generation Benchmarks]]` — specifically the "agentic inference + training co-design" pattern. Primary cs.AI but cross-listed to cs.GR.
- **Status:** new

### 7. Medial Axis Aware Learning of Signed Distance Functions
- **Authors:** Samuel Weidemaier, Christoph Norden-Smoch, Martin Rumpf
- **arXiv:** 2604.16512 — https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.16512
- **DOI:** 10.48550/arXiv.2604.16512
- **Hook:** Higher-order variational SDF learning that makes the jump set of ∇SDF (the medial axis) explicit via an Ambrosio–Tortorelli phase-field approximation. Enforces linear growth along gradients away from the discontinuity set; implemented with two neural nets (SDF + phase field) on unoriented point clouds.
- **Why matched:** Direct companion to `[[Graphics - Ray Marching]]` — classical SDF theory meeting learned SDF practice.
- **Status:** new

### 8. Chatting about Upper-Body Expressive Human Pose and Shape Estimation (CoEvoer)
- **Authors:** Yuxiang Zhao, Wei Huang, Yujie Song, Liu Wang, Huan Zhao
- **arXiv:** 2604.17959 — https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.17959
- **DOI:** 10.48550/arXiv.2604.17959
- **Hook:** One-stage cross-dependency transformer for upper-body EHPS; torso provides global semantic/positional priors that guide finer face/hand estimation, and vice versa. Claims SOTA on upper-body benchmarks with strong wild-image generalization.
- **Why matched:** AR/VR applications — slots next to World Mouse, AppSW, and the AI+XR thread (expressive avatar = XR input modality).
- **Status:** new

### 9. MESA: A Training-Free Multi-Exemplar Deep Framework for Restoring Ancient Inscription Textures
- **Authors:** Vasileios Toulatzis, Sofia Theodoridou, Ioannis Fudos
- **arXiv:** 2604.17390 — https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.17390
- **DOI:** 10.48550/arXiv.2604.17390
- **Hook:** Training-free restoration driven by VGG19 Gram-matrix exemplar style matching; layer-wise contribution weights derived from OCR-estimated character widths bias filters toward letter-scale features.
- **Why matched:** Niche but clever — an exemplar-driven, no-training pipeline useful as a contrast to the field's usual "train a bigger model" reflex.
- **Status:** new

### 10. UGD: An Unsupervised Geometric Distance for Evaluating Real-world Noisy Point Cloud Denoising
- **Authors:** Zhiyong Su, Jincan Wu, Yonghui Liu, Zheng Li, Weiqing Li
- **arXiv:** 2604.16976 — https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.16976
- **DOI:** 10.48550/arXiv.2604.16976
- **Hook:** Patch-wise Gaussian Mixture Model learned over clean-cloud features becomes the unsupervised reference for evaluating denoising on real-world noisy clouds (where ground-truth is unavailable). Self-supervised multi-task training (ranking + distortion classification + distribution prediction).
- **Why matched:** Metric papers are underrepresented in this KB; a reusable eval tool is high leverage.
- **Status:** new
