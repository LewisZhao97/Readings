# Memex

A hypothetical device described by Vannevar Bush in his 1945 essay "As We May Think" (1945). Envisioned as a personal, curated knowledge store with associative trails between documents.

## Key Characteristics

- **Private and personal** — not a public network, but an individual's curated store
- **Associative trails** — connections between documents are as valuable as the documents themselves
- **Active curation** — the owner builds and maintains the links, not an algorithm

## Significance

Bush's vision was closer to the [[topics/Knowledge_Management-LLM_Powered_Knowledge_Management|LLM Wiki pattern]] than to what the web became. The web prioritized public, hyperlinked, algorithmically surfaced content. The Memex was private, actively curated, with connections as first-class artifacts.

The unsolved problem was maintenance — who does the work of keeping trails current and connections accurate? [[entities/AI-Andrej_Karpathy|Karpathy]] argues LLMs finally solve this: they handle the bookkeeping that makes personal knowledge stores sustainable.
